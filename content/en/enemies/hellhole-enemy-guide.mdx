<Section icon={Swords} title="Biome-Specific Combat Strategies">

Each biome in Hellhole introduces specific environmental hazards and enemy synergies. Understanding these interactions is the difference between a successful descent and a failed run. The transition from the Magma Core to the Sunken Ruins marks a significant spike in difficulty, as enemies begin to utilize ranged attacks and area-of-effect hazards.

In the **Fungal Depths**, the primary threat is the Gremlin. These enemies are notorious for their small size and high movement speed. They can quickly close the gap and surround a player, making high-capacity weapons or fire-rate upgrades essential. In contrast, the **Ancient Tomb** features the Brute Wizard and Archers, requiring players to utilize cover and prioritize ranged threats while kiting slower melee units.

| Biome Name | Key Hazards | Common Enemies | Strategy Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Magma Core** | Fire/Lava | Charred Zombies | Basic kiting and headshots |
| **Sunken Ruins** | Acid Pools | Spitters, Skeletons | Ranged prioritization |
| **Fungal Depths** | Low Visibility | Gremlins, Fungal Mobs | High fire rate, crowd control |
| **Ancient Tomb** | Tight Corridors | Brute Wizards, Archers | Cover usage, target priority |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Alert">
Acid spit from enemies like the Spitter does not just deal impact damage; it leaves a lingering pool on the ground. Standing in these pools will rapidly deplete your HP, so always keep moving.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Mastering the Floor Loop">

To reach the boss and descend further, you must master the rhythmic loop of exploration and combat. Each floor follows a predictable structure, but the RNG elements of treasure rooms and upgrade choices require adaptability.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Room Clear">
    Enter the floor and identify the enemy types. Use the environment to funnel melee mobs like Charred Zombies into a chokepoint. Keep your distance and focus on headshots to conserve time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Treasure & Shop Management">
    After clearing a room, scan for silver and gold chests. These provide the credits needed for mid-run upgrades. Visit the shop to purchase **Big Speed** or **Max HP** boosts before moving to the next combat zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Upgrade Selection">
    When you encounter an upgrade room, choose based on your current weakness. If you are struggling to kill mobs quickly, prioritize **Crit Damage** or **Fire Rate**. If you are taking too much damage, take **Magazine Size** to trigger your reload heals more often.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Descent">
    Once all mandatory rooms are cleared, gather your crew at the elevator. Ensure everyone is healed up, as the transition between biomes often involves a drop that can be disorienting if enemies spawn immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Economy Tip">
Don't hoard your coins. Credits earned during a run are meant to be spent at the floor shops. Prioritize **Movement Speed** first, as it allows you to dodge almost every projectile in the game.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Encounter: The Ancient Tomb Guardian">

The final challenge of a standard run is the floor boss. In the Ancient Tomb, the boss fight takes place in a confined arena, making movement and spatial awareness critical. The boss's health scales based on your party size; for example, a solo player will face a boss with approximately **4,000 HP**.

| Attack Phase | Mechanic | How to Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: Melee** | Direct pursuit and swings | Maintain a circular kiting path |
| **Phase 2: Acid** | Projectiles that leave floor hazards | Watch the ground, avoid corners |
| **Phase 3: Enrage** | Increased speed and attack frequency | Use speed boosts, prioritize dodging |

The key to this fight is never standing still. The boss frequently spits acid that lingers on the floor, effectively shrinking the usable arena over time. If you have the **Reload Heals** perk, use the intervals between boss attacks to fire and reload to keep your health topped off.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Avoid getting cornered in the boss arena. If the boss drops an acid pool at the only exit of a corner, you will be forced to take massive damage to escape. Always stay in the center or orbit the perimeter.
</Callout>

</Section>
