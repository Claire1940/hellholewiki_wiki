<Section icon={Swords} title="Early Floor Bosses & Strategies">

The early floors of *Hellhole* introduce players to the fundamental mechanics of boss combat. These fights focus on basic positioning, recognizing visual and auditory cues, and managing your ammunition reserves efficiently. 

### The Screecher (Floor 5)
The Screecher is the first major wall for new players. This boss relies heavily on speed and disruptive sonic attacks that can distort your screen and disorient your movement. The key to this fight is utilizing the stone pillars scattered around the arena to block the linear sonic blasts. When the boss tilts its head back and emits a bright yellow glow from its mouth, immediately dodge behind cover.

### The Abyssal Warden (Floor 10)
The Abyssal Warden is a slow but incredibly powerful brute that wields dual stone clubs. Its attacks deal massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage, making close-range combat highly risky. Keep your distance and bait its heavy ground slam. Once the Warden slams its clubs into the ground, it will remain immobilized for several seconds, exposing the glowing weak point on its back.

| Boss | Key Attack | Visual Cue | Best Counter Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Screecher | Sonic Blast | Head tilts back, glowing mouth | Run laterally to avoid the linear shockwave path |
| The Screecher | Frenzied Rush | Sharp screeching sound | Deploy a quick dash or jump behind solid cover |
| The Abyssal Warden | Ground Slam | Lifts heavy dual clubs | Jump right before impact to avoid ground shockwaves |
| The Abyssal Warden | Dark Spores | Shakes back spikes | Keep distance and shoot the spores before they explode |

To survive these early encounters, you must bring a balanced loadout that allows for both mobility and consistent damage output.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mid-Tier Floor Bosses & Advanced Tactics">

As you descend past Floor 10, the environment itself becomes a hazard. The mid-tier bosses utilize elemental damage, summon minor minions to distract you, and alter the arena layout mid-fight. 

### The Hellfire Amalgam (Floor 15)
The Hellfire Amalgam is an elemental demon that controls the surrounding magma. Throughout the fight, the boss will cause random lava geysers to erupt from the floor, significantly reducing the safe standing areas in the arena. To defeat the Amalgam, players must utilize cryo-infused weaponry to temporarily freeze its outer armor, allowing standard physical damage to penetrate its core.

| Boss | Elemental Weakness | Recommended Weapon | Key Crafting Drop |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hellfire Amalgam | Frost / Cryo | Cryo-Infused Rifle | Obsidian Core (Used for Tier 4 armor upgrades) |
| Plague Bringer | Fire / Incendiary | Flame Spitter | Putrid Gland (Used for high-toxicity ammo crafting) |
| Void Lurker | Light / Energy | Laser Carbine | Void Shard (Required to unlock advanced weapon scopes) |

When fighting the Plague Bringer, always clear the toxic spore clouds it leaves behind. Standing in these clouds applies a stacking damage-over-time poison debuff that can quickly deplete your health bar. Use incendiary weapons to detonate the gas clouds from a safe distance.

<Callout type="warning" title="Environmental Hazards">
Mid-tier boss arenas often feature moving platforms or shrinking safe zones. Always prioritize positioning and footwork over dealing maximum damage.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Deep Floor Challenges & Final Demon Bosses">

The deepest sectors of *Hellhole* host the most terrifying entities. The final boss encounters are multi-phase endurance tests that require precise execution of advanced combat mechanics, quick weapon swapping, and exceptional movement coordination.

The Archdemon Prime on Floor 20 is the ultimate challenge in the game. This encounter combines all previously learned mechanics, including tracking projectiles, summoned hordes, and environmental hazards that change dynamically as the boss's health declines.

  <Step num="2" title="Phase Two: Clear the Summoned Horde">

  <Step num="4" title="Phase Four: Target the Core">

  </Step>
