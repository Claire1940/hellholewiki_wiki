The rank system allows you to equip specific perks before a run starts. These are not RNG-dependent but are earned through consistent play. For instance, reaching Rank 35 unlocks the ability for bullets to drill through enemies, hitting a second target—a game-changer for crowd control.

| Rank | Perk Name | Effect Description |
|------|-----------|--------------------|

| **35** | Piercing Rounds | Bullets drill through and hit a second enemy |

<Callout type="tip" title="Early Game Recommendation">

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="In-Run Combat Upgrades and RNG">

Every time you enter a floor, you encounter Upgrade Rooms. These provide three random buffs of varying rarities (Common, Rare, Epic). Choosing the right combination is what determines if you can reach Floor 7 and beyond.

| Upgrade Type | Rarity | Effect | Strategy Note |
|--------------|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Deadly Crits** | Rare | +25% Critical Hit Chance | Best paired with high fire rate |
| **Heavy Rounds** | Rare | +16% Flat Damage Increase | Essential for late-game scaling |
| **Extended Mag** | Common | +5 Magazine Capacity | Reduces downtime during reloads |
| **Adrenaline** | Epic | Increased Fire Rate & Speed | The top choice for solo players |
| **Accuracy** | Common | Reduced Bullet Spread | Only useful for long-range rooms |

When selecting upgrades, focus on a "build" rather than picking random high-rarity items. If you have already invested in Critical Hit chance, look for Fire Rate upgrades to maximize the number of crits you land per second.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Damage Base">
    Pick at least one flat damage upgrade in the first two floors to ensure you aren't overwhelmed by basic zombies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utility and Speed">
    By floor 3, look for Movement Speed or Magazine Size. The Gremlin enemies move fast, and you need the mobility to kite them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Late Game Crit Stacking">
    In the Fungal Depths and beyond, stack Critical Hit chance to melt the increased health pools of the Withered enemies.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="The Reload Synergy">
If you are using the **Finishing Reload** perk, prioritize **Magazine Size** and **Fire Rate** upgrades. This allows you to dump your mag quickly and trigger the 5 HP heal more frequently, making you nearly immortal in long waves.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Shop Items and Treasure Rooms">

Coins earned within a run are spent at the Shop, which appears periodically between floors. Unlike Gears, these coins do not carry over, so you should spend them aggressively to increase your immediate survival chances.

| Shop Item | Cost | Benefit |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Minor Speed** | 50 Coins | Small boost to movement |
| **Big Speed** | 150 Coins | Significant mobility increase |
| **Max HP Up** | 200 Coins | Increases total health pool |
| **Full Heal** | 100 Coins | Restores all current health |

Treasure Rooms are vital for funding these shop purchases. Always clear the Treasure Room before descending, as the gold found there can be the difference between buying a Max HP upgrade or entering a boss fight with low health.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Enemy Bestiary and Boss Tactics">

As you descend through the Magma Core, Sunken Ruins, and Fungal Depths, the enemy variety increases. Each enemy type requires a different tactical approach to avoid taking unnecessary damage.

| Enemy Name | Primary Threat | Counter Strategy |
|------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Gremlin** | High Speed, Small Hitbox | Use high fire rate or splash damage |
| **Spitter** | Ranged Acid Pools | Constant movement; do not stand still |
| **Brute Wizard** | High HP, Magic Spells | Focus fire and interrupt casting |
| **Charred Zombie** | Melee Swarm | Backpedal while shooting |

The final boss encounter in the Ancient Tomb is a test of your movement speed. The arena is small, and the boss frequently places poison or acid on the ground. You must maintain a circular kiting pattern, only stopping to reload when you are in a clear zone.

<Callout type="warning" title="Acid Hazards">
Acid pools left by Spitters and Bosses deal damage over time. If you have the speed buff, you can jump over small pools, but it is always safer to path around them, even if it means taking a longer route to the exit.
</Callout>
