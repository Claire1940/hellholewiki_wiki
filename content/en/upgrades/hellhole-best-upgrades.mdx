<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapon Armory and Combat Evolution">

Your starting pistol is reliable, but to survive deeper layers like the Ancient Tomb or Crystal Place, you must invest in the Armory. The weapons available for purchase with Gears offer significantly higher RPM (Revolutions Per Minute), larger magazines, and unique passive effects.

| Weapon Name | Cost (Gears) | Primary Benefit | Unique Passive |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Default Pistol** | Free | Unlimited Ammo | None |
| **Lava Weapon** | 5,000 | High Base Damage | **Burn (Legendary)** |
| **Frozen Blaster** | 3,500 | Crowd Control | Slow Effect |
| **Void Rifle** | 7,500 | Rapid Fire Rate | Energy Chain |

Choosing the right weapon is the foundation of the **hellhole best upgrades** strategy. While the Lava weapon is expensive, the burn passive provides tick damage that is invaluable during boss fights where you spend most of your time dodging rather than shooting.

<Callout type="warning" title="Ammo Management">
Even with the "Unlimited Ammo" mechanic, every weapon has a magazine limit. Upgrading your magazine size in-run is critical for weapons with high fire rates to prevent being caught in a reload animation during a Gremlin swarm.
</Callout>

<Section icon={TrendingUp} title="In-Run Upgrade Priority Tier List">

During your descent, you will encounter Upgrade Rooms offering a choice of three random buffs. These are temporary for the current run but are the primary way you scale your power to match the increasing health pools of enemies.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Game: Damage Scaling">
    In the first three floors (Magma Core, Sunken Ruins), focus purely on **Damage** and **Crit Chance**. You need to one-shot basic zombies to clear rooms quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mid Game: Utility and Speed">
    By the Fungal Depths, enemies like Gremlins become faster. Pick **Move Speed** or **Magazine Size** to maintain distance and fire for longer durations.

  <Step num="3" title="Late Game: Precision and Survival">

<Section icon={Target} title="Floor Progression and Enemy Weaknesses">

Understanding where you are in the pit helps you decide which **hellhole best upgrades** to pick. Each layer introduces new mechanics that require specific counter-upgrades.

| Floor Name | Key Enemy | Required Stat | Danger Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Magma Core** | Charred Zombie | Damage | Low |
| **Sunken Ruins** | Drowned | Move Speed | Medium |
| **Fungal Depths** | Gremlins | Fire Rate | High |
| **Ancient Tomb** | Brute Wizard | Accuracy | Very High |

The Gremlins in the Fungal Depths are the first major "gear check." They are small and fast, making high Fire Rate and Magazine Size more important than raw damage. If you cannot clear them before they reach you, your run will likely end there.

<Callout type="warning" title="Acid Hazards">
Spitter enemies and certain bosses leave acid pools on the ground. No amount of defensive upgrades will save you if you stand in these; prioritize **Movement Speed** to exit hazard zones instantly.
</Callout>
