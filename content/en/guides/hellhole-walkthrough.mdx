<Callout type="tip" title="Body Positioning Tip">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Conquering the Corkscrew Squeeze">

The Corkscrew is widely considered the most physically demanding obstacle in the entire cave system. It is a narrow, spiral-shaped fissure that forces you to rotate your torso and limbs simultaneously while sliding downward.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Alignment">
    Position your body feet-first with your face pointing toward the left rock face. Ensure your arms are aligned along your sides to minimize your shoulder profile.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Exhale Slide">
    Breathe out completely to compress your chest cavity. Slide slowly into the narrowest part of the constriction using gravity to assist your movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Spiral Rotation">
    Twist your hips 90 degrees clockwise as you feel the rock contour shift. This rotation is essential to clear the internal stone projection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Ledge Recovery">
    Once your hips clear the narrowest point, extend your legs to locate the small landing platform directly below the exit of the squeeze.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Psychological Management">
Claustrophobia is a significant risk during the Corkscrew. If you feel panic rising, stop moving, close your eyes, and focus on slow, shallow breathing until your heart rate stabilizes.
</Callout>
