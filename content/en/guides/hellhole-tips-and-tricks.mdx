<Section icon={Swords} title="Movement Techniques: Mastering the Corkscrew">

The Corkscrew is the definitive challenge of Hellhole. It is a narrow, vertical tube that requires you to twist your body like a screw to fit through the natural contours of the rock. Without the proper **hellhole tips and tricks** for movement, it is very easy to wedge your hips or shoulders in a position where you cannot move up or down.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Feet-First Orientation">
    Always enter the Corkscrew feet-first while facing upward. This allows you to use your legs to feel for the "drop" and prevents your head from getting wedged in a downward-tapering gap.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Exhale Technique">
    In the tightest 12-inch sections, take a deep breath and then fully exhale. This collapses your chest cavity by an inch or two, providing just enough clearance to slide past the "crux" point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The 360-Degree Twist">
    As you slide down, you must rotate your torso. If your left shoulder is hitting rock, twist your body clockwise. The rock is not a straight pipe; it is a spiral.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Clear the Man Trap">
    Once through the Corkscrew, you will hit the Man Trap. This requires a "side-winding" motion where you keep your arms tucked tightly against your sides.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Movement Type | Best Used For | Risk Level |
|---------------|---------------|------------|
| Feet-First | Vertical drops and the Corkscrew | Medium |
| Side-Winding | Horizontal slits and narrow cracks | High |
| Chimneying | Wider vertical shafts with wall grip | Low |
| Exhale-Sliding | The absolute tightest "trap" points | Extreme |

<Callout type="success" title="Clothing Choice">
Wear a single layer of synthetic, abrasion-resistant material. Avoid baggy clothes or heavy jackets, as these will bunch up in the Corkscrew and act like an anchor, pinning you against the walls.
</Callout>
