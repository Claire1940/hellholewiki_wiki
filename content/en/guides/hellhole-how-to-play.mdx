<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Loop and Progression">

The heartbeat of the game lies in the cycle of descent, scavenge, and secure. To master the mechanics, you must follow a disciplined approach to each floor. Every level in the abyss is procedurally generated, meaning the layout changes with every run, but the fundamental logic of survival remains constant.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establishing a Bridgehead">
    Upon entering a new floor, locate the nearest **Safe Zone Hub**. Use your resources to activate the central light pillar, which provides a respawn point and a place to store excess loot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Systematic Scavenging">
    Move outward from the hub in a spiral pattern. Mark explored corridors with **Chalk Marks** to avoid getting lost in the shifting geometry. Collect all visible scrap and consumables.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Resource Extraction">
    Once your inventory is 80% full, return to the hub. Prioritize upgrading your **Oxygen Filter** and **Light Duration** before attempting to find the exit to the next layer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Final Descent">
    Locate the **Extraction Elevator**. This usually involves a "Hold the Line" event where you must defend the elevator from waves of enemies while it powers up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
The environment becomes significantly more hostile during the "Extraction" phase. Ensure all your defensive turrets are fully repaired before initiating the elevator sequence.
</Callout>

Survival is not just about fighting; it is about knowing when to hide. The "Stealth Mechanic" allows you to bypass high-threat enemies by staying in the shadows, though this will slowly drain your sanity. Balancing these two risks is the core challenge of the mid-game experience.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Strategies and Enemy Management">

Combat in the abyss is brutal and often fatal if approached carelessly. Most enemies have specific patterns and weaknesses that can be exploited. Engaging multiple foes at once is almost always a death sentence unless you have high-tier AOE (Area of Effect) weapons.

<Callout type="info" title="Tactical Information">
Use the **Environment** to your advantage. Many enemies are sensitive to light; throwing a flare into a dark room can stun them, providing a window for a quick execution or a safe retreat.
</Callout>

Melee combat utilizes a "Weight System." Heavier weapons like the **Sledgehammer** deal massive damage and can break armor but leave you vulnerable to fast-moving "Stalkers." Lighter weapons like the **Combat Knife** allow for quick strikes and parries but require precision to hit enemy weak points.

| Enemy Type | Threat Level | Primary Weakness | Recommended Weapon |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shadow Creeper** | Low | Bright Light / Flares | Any Melee |
| **The Brute** | High | Rear Weakpoint | Shotgun / Heavy Axe |
| **Siren** | Medium | Ranged Attacks | Sniper Rifle |
| **Swarmers** | Low | Fire / Explosives | Molotov / Flamethrower |
| **The Warden** | Extreme | Environmental Traps | Heavy Ordnance |

When facing a **Brute**, never attack from the front. Wait for its overhead slam, dodge to the left, and strike the glowing pustules on its back. For **Sirens**, listen for the high-pitched hum. They can stun you from a distance, so breaking line of sight is your first priority before closing the gap.

</Section>

<Callout type="success" title="Optimization Tip">
Always carry at least one **Repair Kit**. Weapons lose effectiveness as their durability drops below 50%, and a broken weapon in the middle of a boss fight usually results in a failed run.
</Callout>

Crafting is performed at **Workbenches** found in Safe Zones. You can dismantle unwanted loot to gain components for high-tier recipes. In the 2026 meta, the "Shock-Inducing" augment is highly favored for melee weapons, as it provides a consistent crowd-control effect against the most common enemy types.
