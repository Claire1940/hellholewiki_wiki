<Section icon={Swords} title="Armory Weapons & Perk Selection">

Permanent progression in Hellhole is managed through the Armory and the Perk Shop. Before starting a run, you should spend your hard-earned Gears to unlock more efficient weaponry and equip passive perks that match your preferred playstyle. You start each run with a default pistol, which features unlimited reserve ammo but suffers from a small magazine capacity and moderate damage output.

Unlocking higher-tier weapons in the Armory significantly reduces the difficulty of deeper floors. For example, the Lava weapon, which costs 5,000 Gears, features a legendary passive burn effect that deals damage over time to hit targets. This passive is highly effective for managing large crowds of zombies and chipping away at boss health pools.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="In-Run Upgrades & Shop Strategy">

While permanent perks and weapons lay the foundation, your success in a specific run depends heavily on how you manage your in-run economy and upgrade choices. During a run, you collect temporary gold coins by opening gold and silver treasure chests. Unlike Gears, these coins are lost when the run ends, so you should spend them freely at shops.

Upgrade rooms appear periodically, presenting you with a choice of one of three randomized power-ups. These upgrades can drastically alter your weapon's performance, boosting stats like damage, critical hit chance, reload speed, and magazine capacity.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Gather Coins">
    Open every gold and silver chest automatically by walking past them during wave clears.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose Upgrades">
    Select damage or magazine size buffs in upgrade rooms to increase your overall damage output.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Visit the Shop">
    Spend accumulated coins on speed boosts and max HP upgrades to bolster your survivability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Heal Up">
    Use Finishing Reload or purchase full heals at the shop before descending to the next floor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Balancing your offensive and defensive upgrades is crucial. While stacking damage allows you to clear waves faster, neglecting your movement speed and maximum health will leave you vulnerable on deeper floors where enemies move faster and hit much harder.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Enemy Bestiary & Combat Tactics">

The deeper layers of the pit host a variety of aggressive creatures. Understanding their movement patterns, attack styles, and threat levels is key to maintaining your health pool and completing runs efficiently. Enemies range from slow-moving melee units to agile ranged attackers that can quickly disrupt your positioning.

Gremlins are among the most dangerous common enemies due to their small hitboxes and rapid movement speed. They often spawn in packs and can quickly surround an unprepared player. Ranged units like Spitters and Archers must also be prioritized, as their projectiles can force you out of safe firing positions.

| Enemy Type | Attack Style | Threat Level | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Charred Zombie | Slow melee strikes | Low | Low |
| Spitter | Ranged acid spit | Medium | High |
| Gremlin | Rapid melee rush | High | Critical |
| Brute Wizard | Area-of-effect spells | High | High |
| Archer Skeleton | Ranged physical arrows | Medium | Medium |

When dealing with mixed waves, always use your movement speed to kite the slower melee units while focusing your fire on fast-moving Gremlins and ranged Spitters. Keeping a clear line of sight and avoiding dead ends in the terrain will prevent you from being cornered.

<Callout type="info" title="Target Prioritization">
If you are overwhelmed by a mixed wave, focus on eliminating Spitters first. Their acid projectiles leave persistent ground hazards that restrict your movement, making it significantly harder to dodge other melee attackers.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Boss Fight Walkthrough & Solo Strategies">

At the end of your descent, you will enter a small, restricted arena to face the floor boss. This encounter is a true test of your build's damage output and your movement mechanics. The boss's total health pool scales dynamically based on your party size, making solo attempts challenging but highly rewarding if executed correctly.

The boss utilizes several area-of-effect attacks, regularly spitting poison pools that cover large portions of the arena floor. Because the fighting space is small, standing still is highly discouraged. You must maintain constant circular movement around the outer edges of the arena to avoid these hazards while continuously firing at the boss.

During the fight, focus on kiting the boss in a wide circle. When the boss prepares to spit poison, quickly change direction or expand your circle to avoid the incoming projectile. If you are playing solo, remember that you do not have teammates to draw aggression, meaning you must manage your reload windows carefully to avoid getting caught flat-footed.

<Callout type="warning" title="Solo Scaling">
While the boss's health is lower during solo runs, the lack of teammate revives means a single mistake can end your run. Do not greed for damage; prioritize dodging attacks and clearing any minor minions that spawn during the fight.
</Callout>
