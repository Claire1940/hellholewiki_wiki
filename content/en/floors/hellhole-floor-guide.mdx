<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mastery and Enemy Bestiary">

Combat in hellhole is a fast-paced dance between reloading and repositioning. While the default pistol offers unlimited ammo, its 12-round magazine requires frequent pauses. Success on higher floors depends on identifying high-threat targets immediately. Enemies like Gremlins are notorious for their small hitboxes and high movement speed, making them a priority in crowded rooms.

| Enemy Type | Attack Style | Threat Level | Best Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gremlins** | Melee/Swarm | High | Rapid Fire / Movement Speed |
| **Spitter Skeletons** | Ranged/Acid | Medium | Side-strafing, High Accuracy |
| **Brute Wizard** | Magic/Heavy | Very High | Critical Hits, Cover Usage |
| **Charred Zombie** | Melee | Low | Backpedaling, Headshots |
| **Withered Spirit** | Ethereal/Fast | Medium | Area of Effect, High RPM |

<Callout type="warning" title="Acid Hazards">
Spitter enemies leave glowing green acid pools on the ground. These deal damage over time and can quickly end a run if you are cornered in a small treasure room.
</Callout>

The bestiary (BC Area) in the lobby tracks your encounters. If you haven't seen a specific enemy, such as the Drowned or Frozen variants, you likely haven't reached their respective floors yet. Consistent progression is the only way to fill out your collection and earn rank experience.

<Section icon={Target} title="Resource Management: Shops and Treasure Rooms">

Gears are your permanent currency, while Coins are your run-specific currency. Managing your Coins effectively within a single run is the core of the **hellhole floor guide** strategy. Treasure rooms are marked on your HUD and should never be skipped. They contain gold and silver chests that provide the bulk of your spending power.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Coin Accumulation">
    Clear the first two floors of Magma Core. Focus on opening every chest. Do not spend coins in the first shop unless you need an immediate heal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Speed and Health Investment">
    In the second or third shop, prioritize **Big Speed** and **Max HP**. Speed allows you to kite Gremlins, while Max HP increases the ceiling for your reload-healing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Late-Game Refinement">
    By Floor 5 (Ancient Tomb), your coins should go toward **Magazine Size** and **Accuracy**. These stats ensure you don't get overwhelmed by high-density waves.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Shop Mechanics">
Shops appear before every descent and sometimes within floors. They offer a rotating selection of buffs. If you have over 1,500 coins, buy out the Speed and Health upgrades immediately.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Strategy: The Final Descent">

The boss fight in hellhole occurs in a confined arena after several floors of descent. The difficulty and health of the boss scale based on your party size. For a solo player, the boss typically has around 4,000 HP. The arena is small, making movement speed your most valuable asset.

**Key Boss Tactics:**
- **Kiting**: Never stop moving. The boss often leaves poison or acid trails that restrict the playable area.
- **Magazine Discipline**: Don't empty your clip if the boss is about to launch a ground-based attack. You need to be able to fire while moving.
- **Health Management**: Use the reload-heal perk by firing a single shot and reloading if your health drops below 50%.

| Boss Attack | Visual Cue | How to Dodge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acid Spit** | Boss leans back | Strafe in a wide circle |
| **Ground Slam** | Boss jumps up | Move to the opposite side of the arena |
| **Summon Minions** | Boss roars | Focus the small adds before the boss |

<Callout type="warning" title="Solo Vulnerability">
If you are playing solo and get downed, you can revive using Robux or by watching an ad. However, it is better to play cautiously and rely on your speed buffs.
</Callout>
