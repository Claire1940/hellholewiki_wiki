Understanding the layout of the cave is the core of any **hellhole map guide**. The system is divided into several distinct "rooms," each with its own character and difficulty level. The transition between these rooms often involves a "squeeze" or a "drop."

| Room Name | Difficulty | Key Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Party Room** | Easy | Large open space, good for staging |
| **The Attic** | Hard | Very narrow, requires intense crawling |
| **Main Hall** | Medium | Crystalline rocks, shiny surfaces |
| **Hall of Faces** | Hard | Clay carvings, site of the main drop |

The **Hall of Faces** is perhaps the most iconic location. It is named for the numerous faces sculpted into the clay walls by decades of visitors. To reach it, you must navigate the vertical section of the cave, which requires horizontal movement followed by a sharp vertical transition.

The **Buddha Room** serves as a deep-cave sanctuary. Explorers often leave "time capsules" or small tokens here. In 2026, visitors reported finding sealed containers that still retain the scents of the surface, such as "classic bubble yum," providing a surreal sensory experience deep underground.
