<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons Guide and Combat Strategy">

Combat in the **hellhole game** requires a balance of aggression and caution. Your starting equipment is meager, but the upgrade path allows for specialized builds. Whether you prefer a "Glass Cannon" approach or a "Tanky Survivor" setup, your choice of weapons will define your run.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Loadout Selection">
    Start with the standard-issue rifle. It has balanced stats and common ammo types, making it the most reliable tool for the first three floors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Resource Accumulation">
    Focus on "clean kills" (headshots) to maximize money drops. Avoid using grenades on single targets; save them for the swarms that appear near elevator gates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Strategic Upgrading">
    Visit the Upgrade Station after Floor 3. Prioritize "Magazine Capacity" and "Reload Speed" over raw damage initially. Surviving a swarm is easier when you aren't stuck in a reload animation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Specialized Weaponry">
    By Floor 7, transition into a secondary weapon like the Shotgun for close-range demon encounters or the Sniper for picking off "Screamers" from a distance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Shotgun** | Close-quarters / Bosses | Very High | Moderate |
| **SMG** | Mob clearing | Low (High DPS) | Easy |
| **Sniper** | Long-range threats | Extreme | Hard |
| **Pistol** | Emergency backup | Low | Moderate |

<Callout type="info" title="Ammo Management">

Investing in the "Scavenger" tree early on is highly recommended. By finding more resources in the early floors, you can afford high-tier weapon upgrades much faster, making the late-game descent significantly smoother.

<Callout type="tip" title="Upgrade Priority">
If you are struggling with survival, focus entirely on **Stamina** upgrades. In Hellhole, being able to outrun a threat is often more valuable than being able to kill it quickly.
</Callout>
